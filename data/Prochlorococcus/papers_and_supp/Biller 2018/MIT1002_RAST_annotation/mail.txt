Hi Steve, Maya, Paul, Allison and Penny,
 
Firstly, I hope you are all doing well!
 
As we discussed in the past, Osnat (cc’d) is working on integrating transcriptomic data across different Prochlorococcus (and Alteromonas) strains, hoping it will help her interpret the results of her own experiments (and maybe we can then try use such a database to understand what Pro “are feeling” in the oceans). Since she is keen on learning AI methods, she has been using them to collect the fold-change data from multiple studies and put them into something called a “knowledge graph”.  So far she has done this for 19 papers, starting from the earliest microarray ones (Tolonen, Steglich, Martiny papers from 2006).
 
While doing so, we have encountered a few cases where the gene IDs differ between experiments. This problem seems to be especially common for MIT9313, which seems to have been re-annotated several times.
 
Do you happen to have a “translation table” comparing gene IDs for different Prochlorococcus strains? Specifically:
For some reason, the Tolonen 2006 paper (which uses the PMT_XXX ID) seems to have at least 9 mismatches with other annotations.
Maya’s 2025 paper uses numeric IMG IDs (which we would like to translate to the old locus tags for MIT9301).
Steve’s 2018 paper uses RAST contig coordinates (e.g. contig00001_XXXXX_XXXXX) which we would like to translate to the MIT1002_XXXXX format.
 
If you have such translation tables they will make our life much easier…
Thanks a lot
Daniel
